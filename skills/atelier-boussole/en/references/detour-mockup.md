# The mockup detour

When the discussion goes in circles because it is too abstract.

## When it fires

Two turns spent on "it should feel warmer," "I don't know if this speaks to
anyone," "it depends what it looks like." People judge badly in the air and
very well in front of an object.

## What you make

The cheapest thing that settles it:

- a one-page offer sheet, with its headline and three arguments;
- a three-email follow-up sequence;
- a deck outline, one line per slide;
- a clickable HTML mockup of a single page;
- a simple spreadsheet: three pricing scenarios, three columns of outcome.

An hour of work, not a week. The mockup doesn't have to be good-looking or
complete — it has to be **reactable**.

Two or three contrasting versions beat one polished one: given a choice,
people choose; given one option, they approve politely.

## Say it's disposable, before you show it

> I'm going to make a quick version so we have something in front of us. This
> isn't a proposal, it's an object to tear apart — tell me what's wrong with
> it.

Without that sentence, the mockup becomes the decision by default. Nobody likes
throwing away something that already exists.

## Make them react

Don't ask "what do you think?" Ask what catches:

- "What's wrong in there?"
- "What would you never write like that?"
- "Which of the three would you show a customer tomorrow, and why not the
  others?"

## File the artifact, keep the decision

The artifact goes in `{root}/docs/maquettes/`, dated — that folder keeps its
French name in both languages, like `relais/`, so switching language never
strands documents you already have. It is never updated and never maintained:
it's a snapshot of a moment. Say so in the file itself, on the first line —
"disposable mockup, made to settle <the question>, on <date>" — so nobody
finds it in six months and mistakes it for a decision.

**The decision is the keeper.** Write it where the current path's memory goes:
in the map on the heavy path, in the decision brief on the light path, with a
pointer to the mockup's path.

## When a mockup isn't enough

Two mockups in, if the decision still won't come, stop. The problem isn't the
fidelity of the object: there is a missing decision upstream — usually who this
is for. Go back to the map and settle that one first.

**Done when:** the artifact is filed in `{root}/docs/maquettes/`, it was
announced as disposable before it was shown, and the decision it produced is
written in the map or in the decision brief, with the artifact's path.
