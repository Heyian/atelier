# The interview

One question engine with a depth dial. Light and heavy ask their questions the
same way; only the count changes.

## The rule, in both settings

**One question at a time.** Never two, never a list of six. An executive who
gets eight questions in one message answers the two easiest and loses the
thread.

**Every question carries your recommended answer.** Reacting is far faster
than inventing in front of a blank page.

> Who is this offer for **first**?
> My recommendation: the customers who already buy from you. You know them,
> they know you, and the first try costs far less than it does with strangers.
> Does that fit, or are you aiming elsewhere?

A question without a recommended answer is a survey, not an interview.

When the answer comes, **play back what you took from it** in one line before
moving on. That's where misunderstandings get caught — not three decisions
later.

## Light-footed — 3 to 5 questions

Only the ones that **change the answer**. The test: if both possible answers
lead to the same decision, the question earns nothing. Don't ask it, however
interesting it is.

After the fifth, you settle it with what you have. Whatever is still unknown
becomes a written assumption in the decision brief, not a sixth question.

## Grilled — until nothing is left fuzzy

You follow every branch to its end:

- "How do you know that?" on anything stated as fact.
- "What if that's wrong?" on the assumption carrying the most weight.
- The number behind the word: "a lot," "quickly," "cheap" are not answers.
- The reverse: "what would change your mind?" A position nothing can move is a
  preference, not an analysis.
- The silence: what the executive never brings up is often what worries them
  most.

Grilled is not hostile. You push on the idea, never on the person, and you say
what you're after: "I'm pushing here because everything else rests on it." A
question that makes someone uncomfortable still gets asked; a question that
humiliates never does.

## Changing intensity mid-conversation

"Grill me on this part," "ok, speed up here": the new intensity applies **from
the next question onward**. You don't revisit questions already asked, and you
don't re-ask what has already been answered.

Confirm in one line — "got it, grilling from here" — then ask the next
question in the new setting. The request can come at any moment, including
inside a heavy-path conversation.

## When they don't know

"I don't know" is a useful answer. Sort it:

- the answer is **findable** → research detour, `detour-research.md`;
- the question is **too abstract** to judge in the air → mockup detour,
  `detour-mockup.md`;
- the answer isn't **needed today** → a ticket in `{root}/docs/tickets/`,
  written now, and you move on.

What never happens: inventing the answer on their behalf and continuing as if
they had given it.

**Done when:** every question was asked alone, with its recommended answer;
the light path stopped between three and five questions; and any intensity
change applied from the next question on.
