# Building an agenda

An agenda that lists topics without saying what each one needs to produce
turns into a meeting that discusses everything and settles nothing. A good
agenda tells everyone, item by item, why it's there.

## Start from the profile's priorities, not a random list

Before building the agenda, look at the Company Profile's priorities. A
topic that touches a stated priority earns real time and an early slot; a
minor one can wait until the end, or a different meeting. Also ask what
this specific meeting is for — a weekly check-in doesn't get prepped the
same way as a quarterly decision-making session.

## Pick up the last minutes' thread

If the executive has minutes from the last meeting, open them and carry
forward whatever action items are still in flight and whatever topics were
left open. An agenda that ignores the last meeting's follow-through forces
everyone to rediscover where things stood, live, mid-meeting. Every action
item not yet closed from the last minutes earns at least a line: closed,
overdue, or still in progress.

## Every item: a time, an expected outcome

For each topic, set a realistic time allotment and name what it needs to
produce: a decision, an information update, or open discussion with no
obligation to conclude. An item tagged "decision" that doesn't have enough
time or the information needed to actually settle something is often
better split in two: an information update this time, a decision at the
next meeting once the missing piece is in hand.

## Who's coming, and why

Everyone on the list should have a reason to be there — owning a topic,
being affected by an upcoming decision, or making the call themselves. An
attendee list padded out of habit stretches the meeting without improving
it; ask the executive to confirm every name on the list earns its seat.

## Before sending

Reread the agenda asking, for each item, whether an attendee would know
what to prepare from reading it alone, with no verbal explanation. A vague
item like "discuss sales" doesn't help anyone show up ready; "decide the Q3
marketing budget — bring current numbers" does.
