# Scaling without burning out

When the question sounds like: "I'm still spending too much time on this,"
"how do I grow this without it eating my whole day?"

**Aim to converge toward minutes a day, not hours.** Heavy involvement while
building the workflow, then time concentrates in the review gate once the
loop is running.

**Add people (or automation) only once the review gate is the bottleneck.**
Not before — and define the trigger signal in advance (review turnaround
creeping up, for example), rather than deciding on instinct.

**Keep lightweight memory.** Per-function notes on what was decided and
calibrated, updated as you go, so decisions stay coherent session to session.
`progression.md` does exactly this for the AI practice itself.

**Typical next practice:** if the executive says they're still spending hours
on something that should run itself, check first whether a review gate even
exists yet — that's usually the missing piece, not the automation.
