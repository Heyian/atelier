# Stopping Claude from making things up

When the question sounds like: "how do I make sure it doesn't invent
numbers?", "it just gave me a price that doesn't exist."

**Keep an approved-facts registry.** Customer counts, prices, quotes — Claude
may only assert what's on the list. "PENDING" beats a confident-sounding
invented number.

**Separate producing from checking.** Ask for a draft in one step, and a
skeptical fact-check pass in its own step — or its own conversation. The
checker verifies, it never rewrites at the same time: mixing the two
reintroduces the same blind spots.

**Red-team big decisions.** Before acting on an important plan, ask a fresh
conversation to attack it — to look for what's wrong. That's advisory input,
never the decider.

**Typical next practice:** if no facts registry exists yet, offer to start one
with the three or four numbers that come up most often in conversation — not
the whole company at once.
