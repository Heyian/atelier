# Getting Claude to ask the right questions

When the question sounds like: "it hits me with ten questions at once and I
lose the thread," "how do I get it to help me think instead of just
answering?"

**Interview, don't poll in bulk.** Have Claude ask one question at a time,
never batched, always proposing its recommended answer — the executive reacts
to a proposal instead of inventing an answer from a blank page. It's faster
than it sounds: reacting beats drafting from scratch.

**Write down every deferral immediately.** When anyone — the executive or
Claude — says "we'll deal with that later," capture it with its context on
the spot; context is freshest right now and never will be again.

**Typical next practice:** if the executive just came out of a rapid-fire
question dump that wore them out, offer to redo the same interview one
question at a time, with a suggested answer each turn.
