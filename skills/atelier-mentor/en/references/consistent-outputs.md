# Consistent, on-brand outputs

When the question sounds like: "why does every answer sound different?",
"how do I make it always sound like us?"

**Separate the brain from the body.** Keep durable instructions, approved
facts, and tone of voice in documents Claude reads every session — not in
your head, not re-explained each time. In Atelier, the Company Profile is the
first brick of that brain; once it exists, every conversation executes
against it instead of rediscovering it.

**One source of truth per meaning.** Tone of voice, key claims, and
preferences each live in exactly one document. If tone of voice is described
in two places, fixing the behavior becomes a hunt instead of a one-place
edit.

**Brand voice as an artifact, not a vibe.** Have Claude extract a voice guide
from what the company has already produced — the site, posts, decks — then
score drafts against that guide instead of judging by feel. A written guide
applies the same way conversation to conversation; a gut feeling doesn't.

**Typical next practice:** if the tone of voice already lives in the Company
Profile but has never been extracted from a real example, offer to do it
once, together, on a document the executive knows well.
